Digital Signature Certificate

What is DSC?
  • Digital Signature Certificates (DSC) are the digital equivalent (that is electronic format) of physical or paper certificates. Few Examples of physical certificates are drivers' licenses, passports or membership cards.
  • A Digital Signature Certificate authenticates your identity electronically and it follows end to end encryption using Asymmetric keys (Public key for encryption and private key for decryption).
  • Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is the electronic format of a physical or paper certificate. DSC serves as proof of identity of an individual or Organization for a certain purpose online / on computer. DSC authenticates and electronic document like how a handwritten signature authenticates a printed / handwritten document. DSC can be used to E-Verify returns filed by a taxpayer and is also mandatory in some cases.
  • A DSC contains information about the user’s name, pin code, country, email address, date of issuance of certificate and name of the certifying authority. The DSCs are usually valid for one, two, Three years. However, they can be renewed before the expiry period.

Who needs DSC?
  • DSC is mandatory for some services / user categories such as e-Verification of returns filed by companies and political parties as well as other persons whose accounts are required to be audited under Section 44AB of the Income Tax Act. (In other cases, it is optional)

    • Advantages of DSC?
    • Authentication: Helpful in authenticating the personal information details of the individual holder when conducting business online.
    • Reduced cost and time: Instead of signing the hard copy documents physically and scanning them to send them via e-mail, you can digitally sign the PDF files and send them much more quickly. A DSC holder does not have to be physically present to conduct or authorize a business.
    • Data integrity: Documents that are signed digitally cannot be altered or edited after signing, which makes the data safe and secure. Government agencies often ask for these certificates to cross-check and verify the business transaction.
    • Authenticity of Documents: Digital Signature documents give the confidence to the receiver to be assured of the signer’s authenticity. They Can Take Action on the basis of Such Documents Without getting Worried about the documents being forged.

    Types of DSC?
    • Class 1 DSC: - Which is Valid for 1 Year, After That We need to Renew It.
    • Class 2 DSC: - Here, the identity of a person is verified against a trusted, pre-verified database; Its valid for 2 Years.
    • Class 3 DSC: - Here, the is the highest level where the person needs to present himself or herself in front of a Registration Authority (RA) and prove his/ her identity; Its valid for 3 years.

    How many DSC can be stored in one token?
    • Only one DSC can be stored in one token.
    • We are giving DSC Token format Only.

      What is a Token?
    • A token in a digital Certificate is a small, password-protected hardware device/ is a pen drive shaped device that stores a user’s digital signature. The token is used to securely sign electronic documents, contracts, and online transactions.
